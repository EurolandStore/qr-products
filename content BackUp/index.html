Description

Leffe Blonde is a distinguished Belgian abbey beer with origins dating back to 1240. Brewed in the heart of Belgium, it is crafted with carefully selected malts and noble hops. Its smooth, well-balanced profile reveals subtle notes of vanilla, clove, and ripe fruit, delivering a refined taste with a gentle, elegant finish.

Ingredients

Water, barley malt, maize, hops, yeast.

Consumption precautions

Contains alcohol. Not recommended for children, pregnant or breastfeeding women. Please consume responsibly.

History

1240

The story of Leffe begins at the Notre-Dame de Leffe Abbey in Dinant, Belgium, where monks brewed beer as part of their daily monastic life.

13th–18th century

Through centuries of wars, floods, and rebuilding, the abbey preserved its brewing traditions, refining recipes and maintaining the authentic character of abbey beer.

1952

The historic Leffe beer tradition was officially revived, bringing centuries-old abbey recipes back to life and sharing them with the world.

Today

Leffe Blonde represents the harmony of tradition and modern brewing — smooth, balanced, and elegant, reflecting Belgian heritage in every glass.

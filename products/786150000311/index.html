Description:

Leffe Blonde is a distinguished Belgian abbey beer, celebrated for its smooth body, gentle malt sweetness, and elegant hints of vanilla, clove, and sun-ripened fruit. Crafted with carefully selected malts and aromatic hops, it finishes clean and balanced, making it equally perfect for an easy evening sip or a food-pairing moment.

History

1240
The story of Leffe begins in 1240 at Notre-Dame de Leffe Abbey in Dinant, Belgium. Monks brewed beer as part of daily life, combining faith, craftsmanship, and early brewing knowledge.
13th–18th century
Through centuries of wars, floods, and rebuilding, the abbey continued its brewing tradition, refining recipes and preserving authentic Belgian abbey beer character.
1952
The Leffe beer tradition was officially revived, bringing the historic abbey recipes back to life and introducing Leffe to a wider audience.
Today
Leffe Blonde represents the harmony of tradition and modern brewing. Smooth, balanced, and elegant, it reflects centuries of Belgian heritage in every glass.
